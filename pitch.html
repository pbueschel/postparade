The problem · Trainer side

Placing one horse is a manual afternoon.

A trainer's core weekly job — finding the right race for each horse — still runs on static PDFs, hand-done eligibility math, and morning phone calls.

  • ✕Condition books arrive as static PDFs on a rolling 2–3 week cycle — nothing matches your horses to them.
  • ✕Eligibility is error-prone math: non-winners ladders (which wins count for N1X vs N2X), state-bred restrictions, preference dates, vet's-list status.
  • ✕Placing a single horse well can take the better part of an afternoon.
  • ✕Recruiting a mount or a spot is phone and text, in a narrow morning window.
~3 hrs

1 missed tier

The problem · Track side

The racing office lives or dies by field size.

A racing secretary's job is to fill the card. Field size drives handle, handle drives the whole business — and the tools to fill are a phone and a spreadsheet.

  • ▸Field size drives handle — more runners means more bet combinations, so average field size is the office's primary KPI.
  • ▸Short fields get cancelled. A race that doesn't fill (min ~6–8) is dropped, and the day loses a betting opportunity.
  • ▸Recruitment is a liaison making calls — a recruiter dialing trainers and agents to coax entries into slow races.
  • ▸Incentives run on paper. Ship & Win checks are administered by spreadsheet and word of mouth.

The differentiator

Draw-in probability.

A trainer's real anxiety isn't "is there a race" — it's "will my horse actually draw in?" Getting into an oversubscribed race is preference-ranked, not first-come. Nobody computes this for them.

We model the preference system (date / stars) + field cap + also-eligibles → a straight answer:

Likely draws in
Recent-enough preference date, field projected under the cap. Enter with confidence.
Likely also-eligible
On the bubble — projected over the cap. Enter as an AE and wait on scratches, or find a softer spot.
Unlikely
Stale preference date against a full field. Don't waste the entry — the engine points to a better race.
Eligibility answers can this horse enter. Draw-in probability answers will it — the question every trainer actually asks, and the signal no existing tool provides.

Competitive landscape

The matching layer is empty.

Every adjacent tool owns data, the office, or the barn — none does two-sided placement. Phone + text is the real incumbent.

Player What they own The gap
InCompass (The Jockey Club) Racing-office system of record at ~90 tracks (Track Manager/RTO) + per-track trainer portal (IRO) Legacy office UI; entry filter shows no "why"; no cross-track view, no draw-in, no recruitment tooling
Equibase (TJC) Data of record + free Virtual Stable alerts; distributes condition books as PDFs for ~65 tracks Alerts say "your horse worked," never "a race your horse fits appeared" — data, not decisions
